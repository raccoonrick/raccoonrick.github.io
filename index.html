Introduction

Since the onset of the Covid-19 pandemic, our lives have been reshaped significantly, and technology has played an ever more central role. With in-person interactions becoming less accessible, our daily screen time has seen a notable increase. This trend has also witnessed a surge in the use of apps as people seek new ways to connect and adapt to the changing landscape.

The problem

Ever since the Covid-19 Pandemic, we have grown to accommodate technology and screens a lot more in our daily life. As it became harder and harder to interact with people in person, daily screen time has seen an increase over the last few years. This is also true for the elderly population of today. As they are at a higher risk of complications, it is especially difficult for them to stay connected. This leads to them not having a reliable way of maintaining their social, physical and mental well-being.

The solution

As the creator of this innovative app, our primary goal was to empower the elderly to navigate the challenges brought about by the increased reliance on screens and the isolation stemming from the Covid-19 pandemic. Our app offers a multifaceted solution to help the elderly maintain their mental and physical well-being. Through secure chat functionality with healthcare professionals, we provide a direct line of communication for their medical needs. Simultaneously, the app facilitates easy, user-friendly contact with family and loved ones, fostering a sense of connection and emotional support by showing their families pictures all in one feed. We've designed this app with the elderly in mind, prioritising simplicity, accessibility, and their overall well-being.

Research and Analysis

Surveys

To gain a comprehensive understanding of our target user base, we conducted a survey focused on our app's functionality and purpose. This survey was strategically distributed across elderly above 60 to uncover potential unmet needs, as well as to identify any pain points in existing solutions. Our primary aim was to gain insights that would guide us in refining our app, ensuring it caters to the unique requirements and preferences of our user base.

From the results of our survey, we found that the majority of respondents own a smartphone and use it daily. The primary use of their smartphones is for communication, followed by entertainment and health tracking.



During the COVID-19 pandemic, a significant number of respondents experienced isolation. They found it challenging to use multiple social media platforms to keep in touch with their family. As a result, a majority of them believe it would be beneficial to have one app that shows all their relatives' posts from different apps.



Interestingly, the difficulty in getting in touch with medical assistance during the pandemic increased across older age groups. This highlights the need for more accessible and user-friendly digital health solutions for seniors. We suspect the decline in the 80+ group is because they are a priority segment in the medical field.

User Research - Personas


To better represent our users and to understand their needs, we created two personas.





By having these two personas, we are able to experience the app through two different perspectives,both having separate needs and wants. As Margaret is an extrovert, she wants to make new friends as well as staying physically active and finding new workouts that seniors can do.

For Larry, he lives with his family at home and is on a wheelchair. So, instead of wanting to stay physically active, he wants to be able to easily schedule sessions and appointments with a doctor or a health specialist. He also wants to stay in contact with his family and be able to easily message them while they are out of the house.

Color Palette

The color palette is particularly well-suited for older individuals, primarily due to the high contrast these colors offer. High contrast color combinations, such as orange and yellow, are beneficial for seniors as they enhance visual clarity and readability. As people age, their eyes may undergo changes that affect their ability to perceive colors and distinguish subtle contrasts. These colors are known for their vibrancy and ability to capture attention, which is especially important for seniors when using apps, ensuring that important information and interactive elements are easily discernible, promoting a more user-friendly and accessible experience for an older demographic.


The Final Product


With the wireframes and the color palette, we were able to create a mockup of the final product. The app will be called "WiseWell" and will allow seniors to be able to meet new people, view workouts made for seniors as well as message and facetime family, friends and doctors whenever needed.

Conclusion

In conclusion, by looking into the problem of seniors being left in isolation during and after the pandemic, we created a mobile app called WiseWell that focuses on offering a way to connect with new people, discover suitable workouts for seniors, access to medical professionals and keep an open line of communication with family that ultimately enhances the quality of life for the elderly during and after the pandemic.
